A Stoic Autopsy of Digital Anxiety

The External Onslaught

Infinite scroll is a torrent of external impressions, each vying for attention. Stoicism teaches that these are 'indifferents'—neither good nor evil. The anxiety arises from misjudging their value, granting them power over our inner state.

KEY CONCEPTS
Externals /ɪkˈstɜːrnlz/
Things outside our control, such as wealth, health, and external events.
"In Stoicism, externals are not to be valued for themselves; they are indifferent to our virtue."
Impressions /ɪmˈprɛʃənz/
Sensory or mental stimuli that arise from external objects or internal thoughts.
"Stoics advise examining impressions before assenting to them, to avoid disturbance."

The Internal Citadel

Fractured attention is a failure of prohairesis, the faculty of choice. By focusing on the present moment and exercising discipline, we reclaim our attention. The mind is a fortress; we must guard its gates against digital intrusions.

KEY CONCEPTS
Prohairesis /proʊˈhaɪrɪsɪs/
The will or moral purpose; the capacity for choice and assent in Stoic philosophy.
"Central to Epictetus' teachings, it is the only thing truly within our control."
Attention /əˈtɛnʃən/
The act of directing mental focus to a specific object or task.
"In Stoicism, attention is a disciplined skill essential for living virtuously."

The Discipline of Assent

Anxiety dissipates when we withhold assent from disruptive impressions. The infinite scroll holds no inherent power; it is our judgment that confers it. Practice indifference to the digital cascade to maintain equanimity.

KEY CONCEPTS
Assent /əˈsɛnt/
The act of agreeing to or accepting an impression as true.
"Stoics emphasize assenting only to accurate impressions to avoid error and passion."
Indifference /ɪnˈdɪfərəns/
In Stoicism, things that are neither good nor bad, but may be preferred or dispreferred.
"Externals are indifferents; virtue is the only good, and anxiety stems from misvaluing indifferents."
